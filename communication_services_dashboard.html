Use of Communication Services

This website visualizes investigate the potential factors contributing to digital exclusion among UK residents based on Ofcom’s Digital Exclusion Analysis dataset.

Overview

The UK communication landscape has shifted significantly, especially for those with health vulnerabilities. This analysis explores how groups with and without health conditions use services such as Entertainment, Utility, and Communication apps. Their digital engagement has implications for social isolation, well-being, and policy design.

Key Insights

  • Health Conditions: People with health issues are more likely to rely on Communication services and also more likely to live alone.
  • No Health Conditions: More evenly distributed service use (Entertainment, Utility, Communication).
  • Policy Implication: Digital communication tools could help reduce isolation for those with health-related limitations.
